## Workflow Corrections

- **Alert content:** The alert email identifies which specific toner is low.
- **No threshold decision point:** No < 5% check needed. Action triggers as soon as Mary or Cyndi begins receiving notifications.
- **Printers vs. Copiers (MFPs):** Data gathering steps differ between printers and multifunction printers (MFPs). These require separate handling in the workflow.
- **Equipment ID:**
  - Copiers: Equipment ID is available in the alert.
  - Printers: Equipment ID is not in the alert — must be looked up in eAutomate using the serial number.
- **Toner install date:** Ricoh ARMS shows the date toner was installed in copiers/MFPs.
- **Shipments:** Toner shipments are managed in eAutomate.

## Notification Infrastructure

Ricoh operates a service called **@remote** (based in Japan) that remotely scans copiers and printers and sends low-toner alert emails on their behalf. Those emails land in the inbox of a **former employee** and are then forwarded to Mary and/or Cyndi.

**Issues:**
- Former employee inbox is an active security exposure.
- Forwarding chain adds a point of failure with no visibility or audit trail.
- Not a sustainable or secure long-term approach.

**Action item:** Investigate a more direct and secure way to receive @remote notifications — options may include RSI, a dedicated shared mailbox, or a Ricoh API integration.
